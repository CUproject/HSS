## Jigging - an overview | ScienceDirect Topics
One of the oldest forms of coarse coal cleaning is jigging. Jigging is the process of particle stratification due to alternate expansion and compaction of a bed of particles by a vertical pulsating fluid flow. A single jigging vessel can treat large particles (up to 200 mm) at capacities approaching 680 tonnes (750 tons) per hour.
